Number of Tornados per Year in Minnesota

The above scatter plot shows the number of Tornado events per year from 1950 to 2019. The data is acquired from the NOAA database for Severe Weather.

The data shows a clear increase in the number of events from 1950 to 2019. The p value of 0 indicates the slope is a good representation of the change in tornados per year.

The r value of 0.59 (or R^2 of 0.35) means 35% of the observed variation can be explained by the linear model.
