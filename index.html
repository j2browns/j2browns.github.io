Project: Storm Severity in Minnesota

In this project we looked at weather statistics for the state of Minnesota to determine if severe weather is trending upward in the past 70 years. We believe severe weather has increased over the years due to Global Warming and based on personal experience.
All of the data in the dataset is considered severe. There are also definitions provided on the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) website with numbers corresponding to what is severe. Therefore we will not have to determine if a storm is severe or not - this is done by NOAA.

Hypothesis:

Between 1950 and 2020, on average there has been an increase in the number of severe weather instances.

Null Hypothesis:

The average number of instances of severe weather between 1950 to 2020 has not changed or has no statistical significance.

Research Questions to Answer:

  • Has Minnesota weather gotten more severe over the years since 1950? 
  • Seasonal Temperature Trends
  • Has number of tornadoes experienced in Minnesota increased?
  • Where have the tornados in Minnesota occured?
